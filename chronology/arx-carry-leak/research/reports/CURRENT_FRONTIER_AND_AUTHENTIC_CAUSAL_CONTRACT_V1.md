## `infrencing.jpg` research contract

The image encodes the core reverse-reasoning map:

```text
unknown key/state assignment
    -> exact local ARX operations and constraints
    -> observed full-round output
```

The same graph is read backward:

- XOR is ternary exact inference;
- rotations and permutations are bijective reverse maps;
- modular additions expose operand/carry constraints;
- feed-forward remains in the standard-output circuit;
- contradiction eliminates a candidate or creates a learned clause;
- repeated compatible chains supply soft evidence until logically exact.

The correct key need not be intrinsically compressible.  It is the candidate
whose induced relations remain jointly consistent across the public equations,
observed blocks, and genuinely diverse views.

Hard rules eliminate.  Soft views rank.  Repeated inference means closure to a
fixpoint across distinct rule families.  Multi-view composition is a
product-of-experts over compression residues, CASI/F8 views, propagation-cloud
size and geometry, contradiction structure, diverse solver trajectories, and
forward/backward/stacked round views.  Correlated renamings are not counted as
independent evidence.

## Known-key to no-key land map

The transfer protocol is:

1. learn signatures on many known full-round keys;
2. choose operators on disjoint known-key validation keys;
3. freeze and hash every rule, header, feature, weight, and ensemble choice;
4. evaluate one unseen target using only public observations or features
   backprojectable from public equations under a candidate assumption.

The generic score is:

```text
L_phi(k) = L_phi(relations(public input, public output,
                           standard equations, candidate k))
```

Roundwise R1--R20 and jointly stacked R1--R20 are separate known-key discovery
arms.  A standard-output claim must explicitly reconstruct any required hidden
intermediate feature from public information plus the candidate assumption.
